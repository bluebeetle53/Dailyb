Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper.

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil (1 tablespoon of salt per liter of water).
  2. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.
  3. Dice the pancetta into small cubes or strips.
  4. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and fry the pancetta until crispy and golden. Leave the rendered fat in the pan.
  5. In a bowl, whisk together 2–3 large eggs and freshly grated Parmesan cheese until combined.
  6. Transfer the hot, drained spaghetti to the skillet with pancetta (off the heat).
  7. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta and toss quickly to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed to adjust the consistency.
  8. Season with freshly ground black pepper and serve immediately.

Species the Chef Could Use:

  • Pancetta (traditional)
  • Guanciale (a more traditional alternative to pancetta)
  • Parmesan cheese (or Pecorino Romano for a sharper flavor)
  • Black pepper (freshly ground)
  • Spaghetti (or other long pasta like fettuccine or bucatini)

Chicken Curry

A flavorful dish made with chicken, spices, and a rich, creamy sauce.

Steps:

  1. Heat 2–3 tablespoons of oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown, stirring frequently to avoid burning.
  3. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and ground spices (such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala) to the pan. Cook for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add chicken pieces (bone-in or boneless) and cook, stirring occasionally, until browned on all sides.
  5. Pour in coconut milk (or a mix of coconut milk and chicken stock) and bring to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook for 20–30 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  6. Uncover the pan and simmer for a few more minutes to thicken the sauce, if needed.
  7. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro leaves.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice, naan bread, or flatbreads.

Species the Chef Could Use:

  • Chicken (bone-in or boneless)
  • Onions (finely chopped)
  • Garlic and ginger (minced or grated)
  • Spices: turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala, chili powder
  • Coconut milk (or alternative creamy base)
  • Cilantro (fresh for garnish)
  • Rice or naan for serving

Vegetable Stir-fry

A quick and healthy stir-fry with fresh vegetables and a savory sauce.

Steps:

  1. Wash and chop all vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Use a variety of vegetables for color and texture.
  2. Heat 2–3 tablespoons of oil (such as vegetable or sesame oil) in a wok or large skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  3. Add the denser vegetables (like carrots, broccoli, and bell peppers) to the pan first and stir-fry for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add softer vegetables (like zucchini, snap peas, and mushrooms) and continue stir-frying for another 2–3 minutes, ensuring they remain crisp-tender.
  5. Pour in soy sauce, sesame oil, and seasonings (such as garlic powder, ginger, or chili flakes). Toss everything well to coat the vegetables evenly in the sauce.
  6. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed. Remove from heat.
  7. Serve hot over steamed rice or cooked noodles, garnished with sesame seeds or chopped green onions.

Species the Chef Could Use:

  • Carrots
  • Broccoli
  • Bell peppers (red, yellow, or green)
  • Snap peas
  • Zucchini
  • Mushrooms
  • Garlic and ginger (fresh or powdered)
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Chili flakes (optional for spice)
  • Rice or noodles (for serving)

Egusi Soup

A rich and hearty West African soup made with ground melon seeds, vegetables, and assorted proteins.

Steps:

  1. Toast and grind egusi (melon seeds) into a powder.
  2. Heat palm oil in a pot and sauté chopped onions until fragrant.
  3. Add ground egusi and fry while stirring to prevent burning.
  4. Gradually add meat stock or water while stirring to create a thick paste.
  5. Add cooked meat (goat meat, beef, or chicken) and assorted proteins (e.g., tripe, cow skin) into the pot.
  6. Add chopped vegetables (bitter leaf or spinach), crayfish, and seasonings. Stir and simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  7. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or rice.

Species the Chef Could Use:

  • Egusi (ground melon seeds)
  • Palm oil
  • Onions
  • Vegetables (bitter leaf, spinach, or pumpkin leaves)
  • Proteins (goat meat, beef, chicken, or fish)
  • Crayfish (ground)
  • Seasonings: salt, pepper, bouillon cubes, and chili pepper

Vegetable Soup

A nutritious and flavorful soup made with assorted vegetables and proteins.

Steps:

  1. Wash and chop leafy vegetables like spinach, ugu, or kale.
  2. Heat oil (vegetable or palm oil) in a pot and sauté onions and garlic.
  3. Add chopped or blended tomatoes and cook until reduced.
  4. Add cooked proteins (chicken, fish, or goat meat) and seasonings.
  5. Stir in the chopped vegetables and simmer for 5–10 minutes.
  6. Adjust seasoning and serve with rice, yam, or plantains.

Species the Chef Could Use:

  • Vegetables (spinach, ugu, kale, or amaranth)
  • Proteins (chicken, fish, goat meat, or shrimp)
  • Tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • Garlic, onions, and ginger
  • Seasonings: salt, pepper, and bouillon cubes

Fried Rice

A colorful and savory rice dish with vegetables, spices, and proteins.

Steps:

  1. Cook rice until firm and set aside to cool.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok and fry chopped onions, garlic, and ginger.
  3. Add diced vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, peas) and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Stir in cooked rice, soy sauce, curry powder, and seasoning. Mix well.
  5. Add cooked proteins like shrimp, chicken, or liver.
  6. Toss everything together and serve hot.

Species the Chef Could Use:

  • Rice (long-grain or jasmine)
  • Vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, green peas, and green beans)
  • Proteins (shrimp, chicken, or liver)
  • Soy sauce, curry powder, and seasoning cubes
  • Garlic, onions, and ginger

Jollof Rice

A classic West African rice dish cooked in a rich tomato and pepper sauce.

Steps:

  1. Blend fresh tomatoes, bell peppers, onions, and chili. Cook until reduced.
  2. Heat oil in a pot and fry chopped onions until golden. Add the tomato blend and fry until the oil separates.
  3. Add stock, spices, and washed rice to the sauce. Stir well.
  4. Cover and cook on low heat until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  5. Serve hot with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or salad.

Species the Chef Could Use:

  • Rice (long-grain or basmati)
  • Tomatoes, bell peppers, and onions
  • Spices: curry powder, thyme, and bay leaves
  • Proteins (chicken, beef, or fish)
  • Seasonings: salt, bouillon cubes, and chili pepper

Chicken Casserole

A comforting baked dish with chicken, vegetables, and a creamy sauce.

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Sear chicken pieces in a skillet until golden and set aside.
  3. Sauté onions, garlic, and vegetables (carrots, celery, peas) in the same skillet.
  4. Mix in flour and cook for 1–2 minutes, then add chicken stock and cream to create a thick sauce.
  5. Combine the sauce, chicken, and cooked pasta or rice in a baking dish.
  6. Top with grated cheese or breadcrumbs. Bake for 25–30 minutes until golden.
  7. Serve hot as a main dish.

Species the Chef Could Use:

  • Chicken (breast or thigh)
  • Vegetables (carrots, celery, peas, and mushrooms)
  • Pasta or rice
  • Cheese (optional)
  • Cream, chicken stock, and flour
  • Seasonings: salt, pepper, and thyme
